Start Early

Take the PSAT in 10th grade for practice, then 11th grade for National Merit consideration.

Retake Strategically

Most scholarships accept your highest SAT score. Plan 2-3 attempts between spring junior year and fall senior year.

Check State Programs

Many states offer merit scholarships (like Florida's Bright Futures). Research your state's requirements early.

Look at College-Specific Awards

Universities often offer automatic merit scholarships based on SAT scores. Check each school's website.
